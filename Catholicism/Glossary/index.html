Catholicism

Glossary

    Absolution:
  • A pronouncement of remission (forgiveness) of sins to the penitent.

    • Ambassadors of Christ:
  • Christians should see themselves as representatives of God.

    • Apostles:
  • One who is sent forth, dispatched.

    • Apostolic:
  • Continues the teachings and mission of the apostles, upon whom Jesus founded the Church.

    • Asceticism:
  • Spiritual effort or exercise in the pursuit of virtue.

    • Assumption:
  • The teaching that at the end of her earthly course, Mary was assumed into heavenly glory, body and soul.

    • Beatified:
  • A deceased person is declared “Blessed” and worthy of limited public veneration.

    • Begotten:
  • Jesus is fully God and not a creation of God.

    • Bilocation:
  • the state of being or ability to be in two places at the same time.

    • Blessed Sacrament:
  • A religious ceremony or ritual regarded as imparting divine grace, such as baptism, the Eucharist and penance and the anointing of the sick.

    • Canonisation:
  • The formal process by which the Church declares a person to be a saint and worthy of universal veneration

    • Catholic:
  • On the whole, according to the whole, in general.

    • Capuchin:
  • A member of a branch (Friars Minor Capuchin) of the Franciscan order that adheres strictly to the original rule

    • Communion:
  • The action of coming forward and receiving the body and blood of Jesus.

    • Conscience:
  • A judgment of reason.

    • Consubstantial:
  • Christ was of one substance with the Father, but it also implies one substance with our humanity.

    • Contrition:
  • The act or virtue of sorrow for one's sins.

    • Covenant:
  • A solemn promise, fortified by an oath, concerning future action.

    • Creed:
  • A symbol of faith.

    • Decades:
  • A group of ten.

    • Detachment:
  • Living more and more honestly before God, in one's own eyes and with others.

    • Dormition:
  • The process of falling asleep.

    • Encyclical:
  • Pastoral letter addressed by the pope to the whole Church.

    • Eucharist:
  • The body and blood of Christ are present in the bread and wine consecrated during the Catholic eucharistic liturgy.

    • Examination of conscience:
  • Reflecting prayerfully on one's thoughts, words, and deeds in order to identify any sins.


    Gospel:
  • A written record of Christ's words and deeds.

    • Hermitage:
  • A place where religious men lived on their own to escape the temptations of the world.

    • Immaculate Conception:
  • A Catholic dogma that states that Mary, whose conception was brought about the normal way, was conceived without original sin or its stain.

    • Incarnate:
  • To take on flesh.

    • Intercede:
  • To go or come between two parties, to plead before one of them on behalf of the other.

    • Intercession:
  • Prayers of petition in which Christians intercede for the needs of others as well as for themselves.

    • Instrumentality:
  • A theological theory that falls under the broader category of the prophetic model of biblical inspiration.

    • Locutions:
  • A supernatural communication to the ear, imagination, or directly to the intellect.

    • Manna:
  • The name given in Scripture for the miraculous food sent to the Israelites in the desert.

    • Maronite:
  • A Syriac Christian ethnoreligious group native to the Eastern Mediterranean and Levant region of West Asia.

    • Monotheism:
  • The belief in a single God.

    • Mystic:
  • The supernatural state of soul in which God is known in a way that no human effort or exertion could ever succeed in producing.

    • Mysteries:
  • Something we enter into, through which we encounter the living God.

    • Novitiate:
  • The state or period of being a novice of a religious order or congregation.

    • Obligation:
  • The moral necessity that we are under of conforming our actions to the demands of our rational nature and to the end for which we exist.

    • Passion of Christ:
  • The sufferings our Lord endured for our redemption from the agony in the garden until His death on Calvary.

    • Penance:
  • A celebration of God's love and mercy.

    • Persona Christi:
  • In the person of Christ.

    • Predestination:
  • God chose us and called us before we were ever made to be His own.

    • Prophets:
  • One who spoke, acted, or wrote under the extraordinary influence of god to make known the divine counsels and will.

    • Purgatory:
  • The state in which the souls of the dead are purified from the consequences of their sins.

    • Purification:
  • To free something of extraneous, contaminating, or debasing matter.


    Real Presence:
  • In the Holy Eucharist Jesus is literally and wholly present—body and blood, soul and divinity—under the appearances of bread and wine.

    • Reconciliation:
  • A Sacrament instituted by Jesus Christ in his love and mercy to offer us forgiveness for the times we have sinned and turned away from God.

    • Relics:
  • The mortal remains of a saint.

    • Resurrection:
  • The rising again from the dead, the resumption of life.

    • Sacraments:
  • A sacred and visible sign that is instituted by Jesus to give us grace, an undeserved gift from God.

    • Sacrifice:
  • To make an offering of; to consecrate or present to a divinity by way of expiation or propitiation, or as a token acknowledgment or thanksgiving.

    • Scourging:
  • A Roman implement for severe bodily punishment.

    • Spiritual director:
  • Function of the sacred ministry by which the Church guides the faithful to the attainment of eternal happiness.

    • Stigmata:
  • Bodily marks, scars, or pains corresponding to those of the crucified Jesus Christ.

    • Tabernacle:
  • Jesus being present in the Eucharist, its Dwelling place.

    • Transfiguration:
  • A change of form or appearance.

    • Transgressions:
  • The actual act of violating the law.

    • Transverberation:
  • A mystical grace wherein the Saint's heart was pierced with a “dart of love” by an angel.

    • Venerated:
  • To give great respect or reverence.
